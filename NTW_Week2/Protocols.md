# Protocols

A network protocol is an agreement about how to communicate. There are protocols on every layer of the OSI stack.
The layer 4 protocols, TCP and UDP, are responsible for transporting internet packets. UDP utilises a ‘fire and forget’ strategy, whereas TCP utilises a ‘Three-way handshake’. TCP is therefore more reliable, but UDP is significantly faster. Protocols in higher layers, like HTTPS and SSH, all have their own use cases. Most of these protocols have a default port number.
